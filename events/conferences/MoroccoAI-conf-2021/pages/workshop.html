Workshop

Moroccan Darija Processing: Natural Language Processing for a Low-Resource Language

The workshop will be divided into two parts: a tutorial where attendees will learn about Natural Language Processing (NLP) for a low resource language, and a practice session where attendees will get to analyze different Moroccan Darija datasets and discuss their findings.

NLP is a field that is in high demand, and where research progresses actively and quickly. Whereas language technology for languages like English and French is highly developed, low-resource languages — like most African indigenous languages — have been left behind and marginalized. There are many opportunities to create new tools for languages with few resources. In this tutorial, we take the example of Moroccan Darija, Morocco’s national vernacular. In this tutorial, we will present the latest tools to deal with Moroccan darija, we will also practice different NLP tasks to different Moroccan darija Datasets.

The participants will first learn basic NLP tools to analyze language in the tutorial. The tutorial will go over NLP notions including text pre-processing and tokenization, n-gram language modeling, n-gram frequency and topic modeling. Then, we will introduce language models to perform different tasks such as text classification, dialect detection, sentiment analysis... The tutorial consists of theoretical definitions and concrete examples in Python. The participants can then move to the practice part of the workshop, in teams of 1 to 5 people. Each team will choose a dataset, and will work on analyzing the dataset from an angle of their choice. At the end of the workshop, the teams will be invited to show their findings in a short presentation.

Workshop Level

Intermediate

Workshop outcome

The participants will gain NLP knowledge for a low resource language in the tutorial. The participants will practice by analyzing text data in the second part of the workshop. The participants will practice analysis and presentation of their results in the last part of the workshop. The workshop is recommended for North African people who aspire to be data scientists, NLP and/or Machine Learning researchers and practitioners, and people interested in computational linguistics.

A certificate of participation for this workshop will be provided for participants upon the presentation of their results and findings.
