Call for Papers

To continue promoting artificial intelligence in Morocco, MoroccoAI is organizing its first edition of MoroccoAI Annual Conference (MoroccoAI 2021) that will be held in December XX-XX, 2021. It will bring advanced knowledge and recent developments in AI for students, researchers and industry professionals.
We invite submissions to the first edition of MoroccoAI annual conference (MoroccoAI 2021) and welcome papers from all areas of machine learning and deep learning.

Technical scope

We consider a broad range of subject areas related to machine learning and deep learning. Topics of interest include but are not limited to:
  • General Machine Learning: classification, unsupervised learning, transfer learning
  • Deep Learning: architectures, generative models, optimization for deep networks
  • Reinforcement Learning: decision and control, planning, hierarchical RL
  • Applications: speech processing, computational biology, computer vision, NLP
  • Probabilistic Methods: variational inference, causal inference, Gaussian processes
  • Optimization: convex and non-convex optimization
  • Neuroscience and Cognitive Science: neural coding, brain-computer interfaces
  • Theory: control theory, learning theory, algorithmic game theory
  • Infrastructure: datasets, competitions, implementations, libraries
  • Social Aspects of Machine Learning: AI safety, fairness, privacy, interpretability
Researchers who have accepted projects in Al-Khawarizmi are welcome to submit their work related to the following topics:

Education Healthcare
Agriculture Environment
Tourism Transport
Industry Justice


Submission guidelines

All submissions must be in PDF format and state the research problem, motivation, and technical contribution. All papers should follow Latex or word templates. Here is an example of a 2 pages research paper following these guidelines. Submissions may be up to 2 pages including all figures and tables, with an additional page for references. Submissions should be in a single column and typesetted using 11-point or larger fonts and have at least 1-inch margin all around. Research works that do not follow these guidelines risk being rejected without consideration of their merits.

Submissions may be previously published, completed, or ongoing. All papers will be peer-reviewed by at least 2 reviewers in the area and accepted papers will be shared on the conference website.
